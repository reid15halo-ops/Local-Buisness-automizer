# Marketing — Content Creation for FreyAI Visions

Create persuasive, on-brand marketing content for German craftsmen. Every piece of content must feel
like it comes from someone who understands the Handwerker world — not from a marketing agency.

## Brand Context

Before writing anything, internalize these fundamentals:

- **Product**: AI-powered business suite for Handwerker (5-10 employees)
- **Brand**: "Industrial Luxury" — Rolex meets Apple. Premium, not cheap. Exclusive, not mass-market.
- **Pricing**: Setup 3.500–7.500 EUR + Retainer 300–500 EUR/month
- **Tone**: Direct, confident, visionary. No corporate fluff. No startup hype. Speak like a trusted advisor who's been in the trades.
- **Language**: German (formal "Sie" for marketing materials, except social media where casual "du" is acceptable for Instagram/Facebook)
- **USP**: "Maßgeschneiderte digitale Infrastruktur" — custom-fit, not one-size-fits-all SaaS
- **Competitor positioning**: We're not Lexoffice, sevDesk, or Meistertask. We're the premium option for Handwerker who want a system built around THEIR workflow.

Read `references/brand-voice.md` for the complete brand voice guide before writing any content.

## Content Creation Process

### Step 1: Understand the Brief

Before writing, clarify:
1. **Platform**: Where will this be published? (Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, Newsletter, Landing Page, Email, Blog)
2. **Goal**: What should the reader DO after reading? (Book a demo, sign up for waitlist, share, comment, visit website)
3. **Audience segment**: Which Handwerker? (Fliesenleger, Elektriker, Maler, Dachdecker, general)
4. **Content type**: Post, story, carousel, long-form, email, ad copy

### Step 2: Apply Persuasion Framework

Read `references/persuasion-toolkit.md` for the full framework. Every piece of content must use at least 2 of these techniques:

1. **Pain → Agitation → Solution (PAS)**: Name a specific pain, make it vivid, present the solution
2. **Curiosity Gaps**: Tease a result or insight without immediately revealing it — create an information gap the reader needs to close
3. **Social Proof / Founder Story**: Use Jonas's background (Steinmetz turned engineer turned tech founder) as proof that this isn't built by Silicon Valley — it's built by someone from the trades
4. **Specificity**: Use concrete numbers, real scenarios, named trades. "Ein Fliesenleger aus Hessen spart 6 Stunden pro Woche" beats "Handwerker sparen Zeit"
5. **Scarcity / Exclusivity**: Only 10 Beta-Kunden. Nicht für jeden. Nur für Betriebe die wachsen wollen.
6. **Future Pacing**: Help them visualize what their business looks like WITH FreyAI. "Stellen Sie sich vor: Montag morgen, Ihr System hat bereits..."

### Step 3: Write Platform-Specific Content

Read `references/platform-guide.md` for detailed formatting rules per platform. Key differences:

| Platform | Length | Tone | Format | CTA |
|----------|--------|------|--------|-----|
| **Instagram** | 150-300 words | Casual "du", visual, punchy | Hook in first line, line breaks, 2-5 emojis (MUST include — platform norm), hashtags | "Link in Bio" / DM |
| **LinkedIn** | 200-500 words | Professional "Sie", thought leadership | Hook → Story → Insight → CTA, no emojis | Comment / Website |
| **Facebook** | 100-250 words | Conversational, community | Question-led, relatable, share-worthy | Comment / Link |
| **Newsletter** | 500-1000 words | Personal, valuable, direct | Subject line → Opening hook → 2-3 sections → CTA | One clear CTA |
| **Landing Page** | Varies | Bold, benefit-driven | Hero → Pain → Solution → Proof → CTA | Book demo / Waitlist |
| **Email Sequence** | 200-400 per email | Personal, escalating urgency | 3-5 emails, each building on previous | Reply / Book |
| **Blog** | 800-1500 words | Educational, SEO-aware | H2 structure, practical tips, internal links | CTA at end |

### Step 4: Quality Check

Before delivering, verify every piece against this checklist:

- [ ] **Hook test**: Would the first sentence stop a thumb-scrolling Handwerker?
- [ ] **Herr Müller test**: Would a 57-year-old tiler understand every word?
- [ ] **Brand alignment**: Does it feel premium and confident, not desperate or salesy?
- [ ] **Persuasion**: At least 2 techniques from the framework are used
- [ ] **Specificity**: Contains at least one concrete number, trade name, or scenario
- [ ] **CTA clarity**: The reader knows exactly what to do next
- [ ] **Platform fit**: Formatting matches the platform's norms
- [ ] **No English**: Zero English words in German content (no "Features", "Update", "Tool", "Startup", "App", "Beta", "DM", "Laptop", "Software", "Link"). Use German: "Testphase", "Direktnachricht", "Rechner", "Programm", "Verweis"
- [ ] **No AI slop**: No generic phrases ("revolutionieren", "innovative Lösung", "skalierbar", "Potenzial entfalten", "optimieren")
- [ ] **No fabricated claims**: Testimonials must be marked "[Beispiel]" if no real beta data exists yet
- [ ] **DSGVO**: No personal data, no tracking pixels in emails without consent mention
- [ ] **Founder voice**: Reads like Jonas wrote it, not ChatGPT
- [ ] **Emoji check (Instagram only)**: Instagram posts MUST include 2-5 emojis — zero emojis fails platform norms

### Step 5: Content Repurposing

When creating content, always offer to repurpose for other platforms. One piece of source content should become:

1. **Long-form** (Blog/Newsletter) → extract key insight
2. **LinkedIn post** → thought leadership angle
3. **Instagram post** → visual hook + short copy
4. **Facebook post** → community/question angle
5. **Story slides** (Instagram/Facebook) → 3-5 slide breakdown

Use the same core message but adapt tone, length, and format for each platform.

## Output Format

Always deliver content in this structure:

```markdown

[The actual content]

---

**Notes:** [Any context about timing, hashtags, images needed, etc.]
```

For multi-platform repurposing, deliver each platform version under its own heading.

## Anti-Patterns — What to Avoid

These are the most common ways marketing content goes wrong for FreyAI:

1. **Generic AI copy**: "Revolutionieren Sie Ihr Geschäft mit unserer innovativen Lösung" — sounds like every other SaaS. Be specific.
2. **Feature listing**: "Rechnungen, Angebote, Kundenverwaltung, Kalender..." — nobody cares about features. Sell the transformation.
3. **Startup-speak**: "Disruptiv", "Skalierbar", "KI-gestützt" — Herr Müller doesn't speak startup. Speak his language.
4. **Desperation**: "Jetzt noch 50% Rabatt!" — we're premium. We don't discount. We're selective.
5. **English words**: "Features", "Update", "Tool", "Dashboard" — use German equivalents always.
6. **Wall of text**: No line breaks, no visual structure — instant scroll-past on mobile.
7. **Missing the human**: No founder story, no real scenario, no personality — could be any company.
